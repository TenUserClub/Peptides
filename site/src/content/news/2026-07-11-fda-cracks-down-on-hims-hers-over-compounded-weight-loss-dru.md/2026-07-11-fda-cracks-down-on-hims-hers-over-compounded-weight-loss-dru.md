The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has increased its focus on compounded weight-loss medications, especially those offered by Hims & Hers Health, according to Alternatifling77. This scrutiny has led to a 14.1% decline in the company's shares during after-hours trading. The FDA is concerned with the quality, safety, and legality of these compounded drugs, which are not FDA-approved and have not undergone comprehensive clinical trials. This action benefits Novo Nordisk, a significant competitor in the weight-loss drug market.

The report indicates that the controversy involves Hims & Hers' plan to sell a $49 weight-loss pill. This pill is a compounded version of Novo Nordisk's Wegovy, available at a much lower price. The FDA's actions highlight the debate about the safety and efficacy of compounded medications, which are often considered cost-effective alternatives to brand-name drugs. The FDA's concerns include possible federal law violations, particularly regarding the use of GLP-1 ingredients in these unapproved compounds.

This regulatory action is part of a larger FDA initiative to ensure drug safety and efficacy, stressing the difference between FDA-approved drugs and compounded versions. Compounded drugs do not undergo the same rigorous testing and approval process as FDA-approved medications, leading to concerns about their safety and effectiveness.

The FDA's enforcement actions could result in stricter regulations on compounded drugs, potentially impacting the market for weight-loss medications. Companies like Hims & Hers may need to reevaluate their product offerings to meet federal standards.
