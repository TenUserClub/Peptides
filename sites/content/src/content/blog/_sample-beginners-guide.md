## What are peptides?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids, the building blocks of proteins. Unlike full proteins, peptides are small enough to be absorbed and used by the body to trigger specific biological responses.

## How peptide therapy works

A practitioner prescribes a specific peptide based on your health goals. The peptide signals your cells to perform a particular function, such as increasing growth hormone, improving insulin sensitivity, or reducing inflammation.

## Common types

- **GLP-1 agonists**: weight management and metabolic health
- **Sermorelin / CJC-1295**: growth hormone stimulation
- **BPC-157**: tissue repair and recovery
- **Thymosin alpha-1**: immune support

## Is it FDA-approved?

Some peptides are FDA-approved for specific conditions. Many others are used off-label or compounded. Regulatory status varies by compound and changes frequently. Check our <a href="/legal/">Laws & legal</a> section for current status.

## What to ask a clinic

Before starting peptide therapy, ask:
1. Is the prescribing physician licensed in my state?
2. What is the source of the peptides?
3. What monitoring is included?
4. What are the known side effects?

## Bottom line

Peptide therapy is a promising but evolving field. Work with a licensed physician, understand the regulatory status, and have realistic expectations.
